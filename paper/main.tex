\title{\textbf{Preference-Optimization Collapse in Frozen Audio--Visual LLMs\\
and a Two-Anchor Mitigation}}
\author{Anonymous}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We adapt a frozen audio--visual large language model (AV-LLM) by training only a small per-modality
transform on its modality-adapter token streams, with a modality-conditional (audio-swap) preference
objective, and we study what breaks and what holds. Applying direct preference optimization (DPO) to this
setup collapses the model: on a held-out perception benchmark its accuracy falls from $0.953$ to $0.013$
and it converges to a near-constant answer. We attribute the collapse to likelihood displacement and the
absence of any constraint on the output distribution, and we fix it with two anchors --- a term holding
the chosen answer's likelihood at or above the frozen base, and an explicit KL penalty to the base on
general inputs. An ablation shows both are necessary: the first alone still loses perception accuracy, the
second alone loses hallucination-resistance. This diagnosis-and-mitigation is our main, reproducible
result. The downstream grounding effect is real but modest: under a leakage-controlled, \emph{paired}
protocol (same test items, McNemar), AVHBench improves on Qwen3-Omni from $0.647$ to $0.707$ --- per-seed
marginal ($p\,{\approx}\,0.06$) but significant pooled across three seeds ($b{=}17$, $c{=}38$, $p{=}0.007$;
bootstrap $\Delta{=}{+}0.060\,[{+}0.007,{+}0.113]$) --- a positive but non-significant trend on Qwen2.5-Omni
($p{=}0.13$), and no effect on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio. A per-token probe shows the learned
transform mostly reshapes the \emph{vision} stream while the audio path stays a pass-through, so conflict
resolution happens inside the frozen LM and the information-bottleneck rate penalty is nominal at our
operating point. All numbers are on $300$-example benchmark subsets; we report confidence intervals
throughout and discuss limitations in detail.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Audio--visual large language models couple pretrained audio and vision encoders to a language model
through lightweight per-modality adapters. They exhibit a modality imbalance: they tend to answer from
the visual stream and under-use audio, including hallucinating sounds implied by the image and ignoring
audio that conflicts with it~\cite{cmm,avhbench}. We ask whether this can be corrected cheaply, by
\emph{freezing the entire model} and training only a small per-modality variational information
bottleneck (VIB) inserted on the adapter token streams.

The training signal is a modality-conditional preference. From a clean clip we synthesize an
audio-swapped counterfactual (the original video with another clip's audio) and prefer the answer
consistent with the \emph{heard} event over the answer consistent with the \emph{seen} event. Because
both answers are scored on the same swapped clip, preferring the heard event requires using the audio.

We report three things. (i)~Applying DPO to this objective, while regularizing only the bottleneck's
information rate, collapses the model (Section~\ref{sec:collapse}); we attribute the collapse to
likelihood displacement and a missing output-distribution constraint. (ii)~A chosen-likelihood anchor and
an explicit KL-to-base penalty prevent the collapse, and an ablation shows both terms are necessary
(Section~\ref{sec:abl}); this is our main result. (iii)~With both terms the downstream effect on an
audio--visual hallucination benchmark is modest and backbone-dependent --- significant when pooled across
seeds on Qwen3-Omni, a non-significant trend on Qwen2.5-Omni, and absent on VideoLLaMA2
(Section~\ref{sec:results}) --- and a per-token probe shows the learned transform reshapes the vision
stream rather than the audio path, with the frozen language model resolving the conflict
(Section~\ref{sec:interp}). We do not claim a state-of-the-art result.

\section{Related work}
\textbf{Preference optimization and its failure modes.} DPO~\cite{dpo} optimizes a log-sigmoid of the
difference between chosen and rejected log-probabilities. This difference can be increased by lowering
both, and the probability of the \emph{preferred} response can fall --- ``likelihood
displacement''~\cite{razin} --- worst when chosen and rejected are near-identical. DPO-Positive~\cite{dpop}
adds a penalty preventing the chosen log-probability from dropping below the reference. Unlike PPO, DPO's
KL constraint is implicit and acts only on the preference pairs~\cite{dpovsppo}.
\textbf{Multimodal preference optimization.} mDPO~\cite{mdpo} reports an analogous failure on multimodal
LLMs (the model ignores the image and the chosen likelihood drops) and adds a conditional term and a
reward anchor; our chosen anchor follows the same principle, with audio as the conditioning modality.
\textbf{Capability retention.} RLHF imposes an ``alignment tax''; explicit KL-to-reference penalties and
pretraining-mixing reduce it~\cite{instructgpt}, and proxy over-optimization past a KL budget degrades
true quality~\cite{gao}; selection should use diverse held-out evaluations~\cite{ivison}.
\textbf{Information bottleneck.} The variational IB~\cite{vib} trades sufficiency against a rate penalty;
Vittle~\cite{vittle} applies a VIB to multimodal-LLM hidden states. We use a per-modality VIB on adapter
outputs and report that the rate penalty, as commonly weighted, is weak (Section~\ref{sec:interp}).

\subsection{Frozen AV-LLM with a per-modality VIB}
The base model is frozen. On each per-modality adapter output (the token sequence
$x\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times d}$ feeding the language model) we attach a VIB via a forward hook:
\begin{equation}
z=\mu(x)+\sigma(x)\odot\epsilon,\ \epsilon\sim\mathcal N(0,I);\qquad y=x+W_{\text{out}}z,
\end{equation}
with $W_{\text{out}}$ zero-initialized so $y=x$ at initialization. The per-token rate is
$\KL(q(z|x)\,\|\,\mathcal N(0,I))$. A bypass flag sets $y=x$, recovering the exact frozen base; we use it
for the DPO reference log-probabilities and the KL-to-base target without holding a second model. Only
the two VIBs (audio, vision) are trainable.

\subsection{Audio-swap preference and the naive objective}
\label{sec:collapse}
For a clip we build a multiple-choice question and an audio-swapped copy (video unchanged, audio replaced
by a different-category clip). The chosen letter is the heard event, the rejected letter the seen event,
both scored on the swapped clip. Let $c_p,r_p$ be the policy log-probs of the chosen/rejected letters
(bottleneck active) and $c_r,r_r$ the reference log-probs (bottleneck bypassed). The naive objective
regularizes only the latent rate $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L_0=-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:naive}
\end{equation}
Nothing constrains the output distribution against the base. Because $\mathcal L_0$ is a log-sigmoid of a
difference, it can be minimized by lowering both letter log-probs; with an always-on adapter and an
input-blind objective the probability mass moves to a degenerate constant answer.

\subsection{Anchored swap-DPO}
\label{sec:method-anchored}
We add two terms:
\begin{align}
\mathcal L=\ &-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)
 +\lambda_{a}\,\big(\!-\log\sigma(\beta(c_p-c_r)-\delta)\big)\nonumber\\
 &+\lambda_{kl}\,\KL\!\big(p_{\text{base}}(\cdot|x)\,\|\,p_\theta(\cdot|x)\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:anchored}
\end{align}
The \emph{chosen anchor} (second term, $\delta{=}0$)~\cite{mdpo,dpop} keeps the chosen log-prob at or above
the frozen base. The \emph{KL-to-base} term penalizes the policy for deviating from the base's answer
distribution on a set of general (non-swap) inputs $x$; because the base is the bypassed bottleneck,
$p_{\text{base}}$ is available without a second model. The general inputs span matched multiple-choice,
audio-presence, visual-presence, and open-ended prompts (the ``broad'' set).

\subsection{Monitoring and selection}
Per step we log the absolute chosen/rejected log-probs (not only their margin), $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$, the
KL-to-base, and the fraction of ``yes'' answers on a balanced yes/no probe (a constant-answer detector).
Checkpoints are saved periodically and selected on the held-out benchmarks, not the training proxy. The
selection rule maximizes AVHBench subject to capability guards (Section~\ref{sec:setup}).

\subsection{Implementation details (numerical)}
\label{sec:impl}
The Qwen-Omni backbones run in bfloat16. VideoLLaMA2 is distributed in float16 for inference but trained
in bfloat16; a differentiable float16 forward overflows attention, so we cast it to bfloat16. The VIB is
computed in float32 (bfloat16's mantissa is insufficient for the squared-mean term in the rate), its
encoder input is LayerNorm-normalized for backbones with large adapter activations, gradients are clipped
to norm $1.0$, and its linear layers are explicitly initialized (the VideoLLaMA2 loader disables the
default initialization process-wide). The Qwen backbones are unaffected by these guards.

\section{Experiments}
\subsection{Setup}
\label{sec:setup}
\textbf{Backbones:} Qwen3-Omni (primary), Qwen2.5-Omni, VideoLLaMA2.1-7B-AV; all frozen, only the
two VIBs trained. \textbf{Training data:} audio-swapped AVE clips (300 pairs, 2 epochs, $\beta{=}0.1$,
$\lambda_a{=}1$, $\lambda_{kl}{=}2$, $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ unless stated). \textbf{Benchmarks (held-out,
$n{=}300$ subset each):} AVHBench~\cite{avhbench} (audio--visual hallucination, overall accuracy);
CMM~\cite{cmm} perception accuracy (PA; correctly affirms present content) and hallucination resistance
(HR; correctly denies absent content); DAVE~\cite{dave} audio--visual multiple choice. \textbf{Selection protocol (leakage control):} each benchmark's $300$ per-example records are split,
by a fixed seed, into a validation half and a disjoint test half; checkpoints (and the recipe and
$\beta_{kl}$) are selected on the validation half and \emph{all reported numbers are on the test half}.
The selection rule is the highest validation AVHBench subject to capability guards
$\text{PA}\ge\text{PA}_{\text{base}}-0.05$ and $\text{HR}\ge\text{HR}_{\text{base}}-0.05$ (relative to
each backbone's base, computed on validation). Reported metrics are therefore on $n{=}150$.
\textbf{Answer parsing.} Free-text outputs are mapped to yes/no by a parser that recognizes negated
absence answers (e.g.\ ``I did not see a tree''$\,\to\,$``no''). An earlier negation-blind parser scored
such answers as unparseable errors, which floored VideoLLaMA2's CMM hallucination-resistance to near
zero; VideoLLaMA2 answers in full sentences, whereas the Qwen backbones answer with a leading
``yes''/``no'' and are unaffected. We corrected the parser and re-derived every metric from the saved raw
outputs; the correction leaves all Qwen2.5-/Qwen3-Omni numbers unchanged, isolating the change to the one
backbone that answered in prose.
\textbf{Development vs.\ test, and paired statistics.} The \emph{design} of the broad anchor was informed
by inspecting CMM perception/hallucination failures on the held-out benchmarks, so we read CMM as a
\emph{development} signal and treat AVHBench as the primary held-out test; the checkpoint, recipe and
$\beta_{kl}$ are still selected only on the validation half, but a residual test-informed-design risk
remains, which we flag rather than claim away. Because base and adapted see identical items, every
base-vs-adapted comparison below is a \emph{paired} McNemar test on the discordant pairs, with bootstrap
$95\%$ CIs over examples reported alongside the seed variance.

\subsection{The collapse and the anchor ablation (Qwen3-Omni)}
\label{sec:abl}

reports the end-of-training (step 150) CMM axes to show the collapse dynamics. The naive objective
(Eq.~\ref{eq:naive}) reduces CMM perception (test) from $0.939$ to $0.015$ by step 150 and the model

mid-training.

\subsection{Held recipe, seed variation, and rate sweep (Qwen3-Omni)}
\label{sec:results}
Because base and adapted are evaluated on identical test items, we report a paired McNemar test on the
discordant pairs rather than comparing two independently-noisy point estimates (Table~\ref{tab:seeds}).
Per seed the test AVHBench gain is $+0.060/+0.020/+0.060$ but only marginal (discordant
$b/c=5/14,\,7/10,\,5/14$; $p=0.06,\,0.63,\,0.06$); pooled across the three seeds it is significant
($b{=}17$, $c{=}38$, $p=0.007$), and a bootstrap over test examples gives
$\Delta=+0.060\,[{+}0.007,{+}0.113]$ for the strongest seed against a base of $0.647\,[0.567,0.727]$. The
selected model is not collapsed (yes-rate $0.62$ on a balanced probe), and the selection is insensitive to
the guard threshold (the same checkpoint is chosen at $\mathrm{tol}\in\{0.03,0.05,0.08\}$). The capability
axes do not move significantly (CMM-PA $-0.030$, $p=0.50$; CMM-HR $+0.060$, $p=0.18$), and we treat these
as development signals (Section~\ref{sec:setup}). The $\pm0.023$ in Table~\ref{tab:seeds} is seed variance
only and understates the per-example noise. Table~\ref{tab:beta} sweeps the rate weight $\beta_{kl}$: the
test AVHBench is comparable across $\{0.01,0.1,1.0\}$ (all $\approx0.70$) while the number of checkpoints
passing the validation guards increases ($2/5,4/5,5/5$), i.e.\ larger $\beta_{kl}$ buys training stability,
not accuracy --- consistent with the nominal rate penalty (Section~\ref{sec:interp}). The apparent
$\beta_{kl}{=}0.1$ advantage under full-set (leaky) selection did not survive the val/test split.

\subsection{Multi-model results}
Table~\ref{tab:models} reports all three backbones under the same paired protocol. On Qwen2.5-Omni
AVHBench rises $0.620\to0.673$, but the paired test is \emph{not} significant (McNemar $b{=}7$, $c{=}15$,
$p=0.13$; bootstrap $\Delta\,[-0.007,+0.113]$ includes zero); perception is unchanged ($0.939$) and HR
drifts down $0.619\to0.583$ ($p=0.25$). The relative guard (Section~\ref{sec:setup}) is needed here
because Qwen2.5's base HR is below the absolute $0.70$ threshold used for Qwen3. We therefore report
Qwen2.5-Omni as a positive but non-significant trend. On VideoLLaMA2 --- which trains once the numerical
guards of Section~\ref{sec:impl} are applied --- no axis changes significantly (AVHBench $\Delta-0.053$,
$p=0.29$; CMM-PA $p=0.63$; CMM-HR $p=1.0$, preserved at $0.92$). Notably the adapter still flips $44$ of
$150$ AVHBench answers ($b{=}26$, $c{=}18$) but with \emph{no net direction} --- it perturbs the vision
stream without an audio signal to land on. This is consistent with VideoLLaMA2's weak, separately-aligned
audio pathway: its audio behavior largely inherits its vision-language base model~\cite{avllm_seehear} and
its audio tokens prune almost entirely without loss of accuracy~\cite{fastav}. Running the per-token probe
of Section~\ref{sec:interp} on VideoLLaMA2 --- a direct internal check of ``no audio signal to exploit'',
i.e.\ whether its audio VIB is also a pass-through --- is the cleanest falsification and we leave it to
future work. We did not sweep DAVE; base DAVE (MCQ) accuracies are $0.380/0.307/0.357$
for Qwen3-/Qwen2.5-Omni/VideoLLaMA2. (VideoLLaMA2's high base hallucination-resistance, $0.92$, is the
corrected measurement of Section~\ref{sec:setup}, ``Answer parsing''; an earlier draft scored it near
zero.)

\subsection{What the trained bottleneck does}
\label{sec:interp}
A forward-pass probe of the held Qwen3-Omni checkpoint reads each VIB's per-token rate (KL in bits) and
edit magnitude ($\|W_{\text{out}}z\|/\|x\|$); Table~\ref{tab:interp} reports averages over 8 clips. The
vision VIB rewrites the vision tokens substantially ($\sim$59\% of each token's magnitude, spread across
all tokens) while the audio VIB is close to a pass-through (its small edit is a constant bias; its rate is
near zero). The grounding is thus achieved by reshaping the vision stream rather than by modifying audio;
because each VIB is per-modality, this transform is unconditional and the conflict resolution occurs in
the frozen language model. The vision rate ($\sim$640 bits/token) is large relative to the loss weight
$\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ applied to the \emph{mean} rate, i.e. the bottleneck is weakly compressed at this
setting --- consistent with the $\beta_{kl}$ sweep, where larger weights stabilize training.

\section{Limitations}
The evaluation uses $300$-example subsets split $150/150$ (validation for selection, test for reporting),
so test metrics are on $n{=}150$; we therefore lead with paired McNemar tests and bootstrap CIs rather than
bare point estimates, and report seed variance separately. The grounding gain is modest: significant only
when pooled across three seeds on Qwen3-Omni ($p=0.007$; marginal per seed), a non-significant trend on
Qwen2.5-Omni, and null on VideoLLaMA2. It is the validation-selected checkpoint, not a monotone training
improvement --- all recipes drift if trained longer. As stated in Section~\ref{sec:setup}, the broad
anchor was designed after inspecting CMM failures, so CMM is a development signal and a residual
test-informed-design risk remains. The information-bottleneck framing is partly nominal: at
$\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ the rate penalty is weak and the learned transform reshapes the vision stream rather
than compressing audio. We audit clip-id overlap between the AVE training pool and the three benchmarks to
check dataset-level leakage. Finally, the audio-swap signal is synthetic, the benchmarks are yes/no-heavy
(the regime where likelihood displacement bites), and the training set is small ($300$ pairs, two epochs);
a larger-data replication and a non-yes/no (open-ended) slice are the natural next checks.

\section{Conclusion}
The robust, transferable result of this paper is a diagnosis and a fix: naive preference optimization of a
frozen AV-LLM's adapter streams collapses the model via likelihood displacement, and a two-anchor loss
(a chosen-likelihood anchor plus a KL-to-base on general inputs) prevents it, with an ablation showing
both terms are necessary. The downstream grounding effect is smaller and backbone-dependent --- a
significant (pooled) but modest AVHBench gain on Qwen3-Omni, a non-significant trend on Qwen2.5-Omni, and
no effect on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio. A per-token probe shows the learned transform mostly
reshapes the vision stream, so the conflict is resolved inside the frozen language model and the
bottleneck's rate penalty is nominal at our operating point. We release the diagnosis, the recipe, the
monitoring, selection, and paired-statistics protocol, and the numerical fixes.
