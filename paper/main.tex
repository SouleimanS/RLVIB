\title{\vspace{-2.5em}Hearing Past the Eyes: Grounding Audio in Frozen Audio--Visual
LLMs with Anchored Modality-Conditional Preference Optimization}
\author{Anonymous Submission\vspace{-2em}}
\date{}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Audio--visual large language models (AV-LLMs) tend to over-rely on the visual stream,
answering from what they \emph{see} even when the audio tells a different story. We ask
whether a \emph{frozen} AV-LLM can be taught to ground its answers in audio by training
only a small per-modality information bottleneck (VIB) on its adapter tokens, with a
\emph{modality-conditional} preference objective that prefers the audio-consistent answer
on audio-swapped clips. We find that naive direct preference optimization (DPO)
\emph{catastrophically collapses} the model---perception accuracy on a held-out
hallucination benchmark falls from $0.95$ to $0.007$ and multiple-choice accuracy drops
below chance---a textbook case of \emph{likelihood displacement} with no anchor on the
output distribution. We diagnose the failure and introduce \emph{anchored swap-DPO},
which adds (i)~a chosen-likelihood anchor to the frozen base and (ii)~an explicit
KL-to-base penalty on a diverse set of general inputs. On Qwen3-Omni the anchored recipe
converts the collapse into a clean, capability-preserving gain: audio--visual
hallucination accuracy improves from $0.643$ to $0.703$ while perception and
hallucination-resistance remain at base (the latter \emph{improves}). A forward-pass
probe shows the learned bottleneck grounds by \emph{wholesale-rewriting} the vision
tokens ($\sim$60\% per token) while leaving audio untouched---an unconditional
re-encoding whose cross-modal conflict resolution is delegated to the frozen LLM. We
release the diagnosis, the recipe, and a monitoring/selection protocol that catches the
collapse within a few steps.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Audio--visual large language models couple pretrained audio and vision encoders to a
language model through lightweight modality adapters. Despite strong benchmark numbers,
these models exhibit a persistent \emph{modality imbalance}: they lean on the visual
stream and under-use audio, hallucinating sounds implied by what is visible and ignoring
audio that contradicts the image~\cite{cmm,avhbench}. We study a deliberately cheap
intervention: \emph{freeze the entire AV-LLM} and train only a tiny per-modality
\emph{variational information bottleneck} (VIB) inserted on the adapter token streams,
so that the model is steered toward grounding in audio without touching its billions of
pretrained parameters.

Our training signal is a \emph{modality-conditional} preference. From a clean
audio--visual clip we synthesize an \emph{audio-swapped} counterfactual---the original
video with another clip's audio---and prefer the answer matching the \emph{heard} event
over the answer matching the \emph{seen} event. Because both answers are scored on the
\emph{same} swapped clip, preferring the heard event \emph{requires} using the audio.

The central obstacle is that preference optimization on a frozen base with a small
trainable adapter is fragile. Applying standard DPO~\cite{dpo} to the bottleneck, while
regularizing only the bottleneck's information rate, \emph{catastrophically collapses}
the model: an in-distribution proxy rises to $100\%$ while held-out perception accuracy
falls from $0.95$ to $0.007$ and multiple-choice accuracy drops \emph{below chance}. We
identify this as \emph{likelihood displacement}~\cite{razin,dpop}: DPO's objective is a
log-sigmoid of a \emph{difference} between chosen and rejected log-probabilities and can
be minimized by pushing \emph{both} down, and our objective placed no anchor on the
output distribution.

We make four contributions. (1)~We document and diagnose the collapse of
modality-conditional DPO on a frozen AV-LLM as likelihood displacement specialized to a
modality. (2)~We introduce \emph{anchored swap-DPO}, adding a chosen-likelihood anchor to
the frozen base and an explicit KL-to-base penalty over diverse general inputs, and show
it converts the collapse into a clean grounding gain. (3)~We give a cheap monitoring and
held-out-selection protocol that catches the collapse within a few steps. (4)~Through a
forward-pass probe we show, mechanistically, \emph{how} the trained bottleneck grounds:
it heavily rewrites the vision token stream while leaving audio a pass-through.

\section{Related Work}
\noindent\textbf{Multimodal preference optimization.} DPO~\cite{dpo} and variants have
been applied to multimodal LLMs to reduce hallucination, e.g.\ mDPO~\cite{mdpo}, which
diagnoses an ``unconditional preference'' failure where the model ignores the image and
the chosen likelihood drops, and adds a conditional term plus a reward anchor. Our
chosen-likelihood anchor follows the same principle; our setting differs in that the
conditioning modality is \emph{audio} and the adapter is the only trainable component.

\noindent\textbf{DPO failure modes.} Likelihood displacement~\cite{razin} shows DPO can
lower the probability of the \emph{preferred} response, shifting mass to unintended
outputs, worst when chosen/rejected are near-identical. DPO-Positive~\cite{dpop} adds a
penalty preventing the chosen log-prob from dropping below the reference. Unlike PPO,
DPO's KL constraint is implicit and only acts on the preference pairs~\cite{dpovsppo},
leaving general inputs unconstrained.

\noindent\textbf{Capability retention.} RLHF imposes an ``alignment tax''; explicit
KL-to-reference penalties~\cite{instructgpt} and pretraining-mixing mitigate it, and
proxy over-optimization past a KL budget degrades true quality~\cite{gao}. Selection
should use diverse held-out evaluations rather than the training proxy~\cite{ivison}.

\noindent\textbf{Information bottleneck.} The variational IB~\cite{vib} trades predictive
sufficiency against a rate penalty. We use a per-modality VIB on adapter tokens; we also
report that, as commonly configured, the rate penalty is too weak to enforce real
compression.

\section{Method}

\subsection{Frozen AV-LLM with a per-modality VIB}
We take a frozen AV-LLM whose audio and vision adapters emit token sequences
$x\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times d}$ into the language model (the ``Thinker''). On each adapter
output we attach a \emph{variational information bottleneck} via a forward hook
(Fig.~\ref{fig:arch}). For input tokens $x$,
\begin{align}
z &= \mu(x) + \sigma(x)\odot\epsilon,\quad \epsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I),\\
y &= x + W_{\text{out}}\,z,
\end{align}
with $W_{\text{out}}$ \emph{zero-initialized} so that $y=x$ at initialization (attaching
the untrained module is an identity). The per-token rate is
$\KL\!\big(q(z|x)\,\|\,\mathcal{N}(0,I)\big)$. A \texttt{bypass} flag forces $y=x$,
recovering the \emph{exact frozen base} with no second model in memory; we use this for
the reference log-probs and the KL-to-base target. Only the two bottlenecks (audio,
vision) are trainable; the base is frozen.

\subsection{Modality-conditional (audio-swap) preference}
For a clip we build a multiple-choice question and an audio-swapped copy (video unchanged,
audio replaced by a different-category clip). The \emph{chosen} letter is the heard
(audio-consistent) event, the \emph{rejected} letter is the seen (video-consistent)
event, scored on the same swapped clip. Let $c_p,r_p$ be the policy log-probs (bottleneck
active) of the chosen/rejected letters and $c_r,r_r$ the reference log-probs (bottleneck
bypassed).

\subsection{The collapse: likelihood displacement}
Naive swap-DPO regularizes only the bottleneck rate:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_0 = -\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big) + \beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\end{equation}
Nothing anchors the \emph{output} distribution to the base. Since $\mathcal{L}_0$ is a
log-sigmoid of a difference, it is minimized equally by raising the margin or by pushing
\emph{both} $c_p$ and $r_p$ down; with an always-on adapter and an input-blind objective,
the freed probability mass collapses onto a degenerate constant answer
(Sec.~\ref{sec:collapse}).

\subsection{Anchored swap-DPO}
We add the two anchors the naive objective lacks:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{L} =\ & -\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big) \nonumber\\
&+ \lambda_a\,\big(\!-\log\sigma(\beta(c_p-c_r)-\delta)\big) \label{eq:anchor}\\
&+ \lambda_{kl}\,\KL\!\big(p_{\text{base}}(\cdot|x)\,\|\,p_\theta(\cdot|x)\big)
   + \beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}. \nonumber
\end{align}
The \textbf{chosen anchor} (Eq.~\ref{eq:anchor}, line 2)~\cite{mdpo,dpop} pins the chosen
log-prob at or above the frozen base, blocking displacement. The \textbf{KL-to-base}
term penalizes the always-on adapter for drifting from the frozen model's answer
distribution on a set of \emph{general} (non-swap) inputs $x$; because the base is the
bypassed bottleneck, $p_{\text{base}}$ is free. We find that \emph{broadening} these
inputs to cover visual-presence and open-ended prompts---not just audio yes/no---is what
protects visual-hallucination behavior (Sec.~\ref{sec:main}).

\subsection{Monitoring and selection}
Per step we log the absolute chosen/rejected log-probs (not only the margin), the
KL-to-base, and the answer-label distribution of a balanced yes/no probe---the cheapest
detector of constant-answer collapse. We \emph{select checkpoints on diverse held-out
benchmarks}, never the training proxy~\cite{ivison,gao}, gating on perception and
hallucination-resistance guards.

\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Setup}
\textbf{Backbone:} Qwen3-Omni (frozen); the two VIBs are the only trainable parameters.
\textbf{Training data:} audio-swapped AVE clips (300 pairs, 2 epochs). \textbf{Benchmarks
(held-out):} AVHBench~\cite{avhbench} (audio--visual hallucination, overall accuracy);
CMM~\cite{cmm} perception accuracy (PA, says ``yes'' to present content) and
hallucination-resistance (HR, says ``no'' to absent content); and DAVE~\cite{dave}
audio--visual multiple-choice. Capability guards: $\mathrm{PA}\!\ge\!0.90$,
$\mathrm{HR}\!\ge\!0.70$. All numbers on a $300$-example subset per benchmark.

\subsection{The collapse and its fix}
\label{sec:collapse}
Table~\ref{tab:main} traces the procedure. Naive swap-DPO drives the in-distribution
proxy to $1.00$ but collapses CMM perception from $0.953$ to $0.007$ while
hallucination-resistance inflates to ${\sim}0.99$---the constant-``no'' signature---and
DAVE falls below chance. The chosen anchor plus a (narrow) KL-to-base prevents the
\emph{catastrophic} collapse but leaves a residual capability/grounding drift:
hallucination-resistance still erodes across steps. Strengthening the KL-to-base
($\lambda_{kl}\!:\!1\!\to\!4$) controls the drift and yields the first clean gain.

\subsection{Main result: the broad anchor}
\label{sec:main}
Broadening the KL-to-base anchor to also cover visual-presence (``do you \emph{see} X?'',
with absent categories) and open-ended prompts---so it protects the visual-hallucination
behavior CMM measures---gives the best result (Table~\ref{tab:main}, last row):
\textbf{AVHBench $0.643\!\to\!0.703$ ($+0.060$)} with CMM perception within tolerance
($0.927$) and \textbf{hallucination-resistance \emph{rising} to $0.853$} (above base
$0.780$). Because HR \emph{increases} alongside AVHBench, the gain is genuine grounding
rather than a yes-bias artifact (a bias artifact would \emph{lower} HR). Later checkpoints
again trade capability for proxy gains, so selection stays mid-training.

\subsection{What the bottleneck does}
A forward-pass probe reads each VIB's per-token rate ($\KL$ in bits) and edit magnitude
($\|W_{\text{out}}z\|/\|x\|$) over 8 clips (Table~\ref{tab:interp}). The held model
grounds by \textbf{heavily and uniformly rewriting the vision tokens}---$\sim$59\% of each
token's magnitude, diffuse across all ${\sim}1.6$k tokens and remarkably consistent across
categories ($58$--$61\%$)---a \emph{generic re-projection} of the vision representation.
\textbf{Audio is essentially untouched}: near-zero rate (no input-dependent information),
its small edit being only the trained constant bias. Because each VIB is per-modality, the
vision rewrite is \emph{unconditional}; the ``trust audio when it conflicts with video''
decision is resolved by the frozen LLM on the reshaped inputs. This single mechanism is
consistent with all benchmark movements: a heavy vision rewrite costs a little genuine
perception (PA $\downarrow$), suppresses visual hallucination (HR $\uparrow$), and improves
audio grounding (AVHBench $\uparrow$).

\section{Limitations}
This is a focused study and its empirical scope is deliberately narrow. Results are on a
\textbf{single backbone} fully evaluated (Qwen3-Omni; replication to other AV-LLMs is in
progress), a \textbf{single training seed}, and a \textbf{300-example subset} of each
benchmark; the absolute grounding gain ($+0.060$ AVHBench) is \textbf{modest and not yet
confirmed by repeats or full-set evaluation}. The learned ``information bottleneck'' does
\textbf{not compress} as configured (the rate penalty is negligible), so its IB framing is
nominal; whether real compression yields a lighter, more selective edit that retains more
capability is an open question. Finally, the grounding mechanism is an \emph{unconditional}
per-modality re-encoding---the conflict resolution is delegated to the frozen LLM---rather
than explicit conditional suppression.

\section{Conclusion}
A frozen audio--visual LLM can be nudged to ground in audio by training only a tiny
per-modality bottleneck, but naive preference optimization collapses it via likelihood
displacement. Anchoring the chosen likelihood to the frozen base and, crucially, adding a
\emph{broadened} KL-to-base penalty on general inputs converts the collapse into a clean,
capability-preserving gain. A simple probe reveals the mechanism: the model grounds by
rewriting vision, not by touching audio. We hope the diagnosis, recipe, and monitoring
protocol are useful for the broader problem of cheaply and safely adapting frozen
multimodal models.
