\title{\textbf{Preference-Optimization Collapse in Frozen Audio--Visual LLMs\\
and a Two-Anchor Mitigation}}
\author{Anonymous}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We adapt a frozen audio--visual large language model (AV-LLM) by training only a small per-modality
transform on its modality-adapter token streams, with a modality-conditional (audio-swap) preference
objective, and we study what breaks and what holds. Applying direct preference optimization (DPO) to this
setup collapses the model: its perception accuracy falls to near zero and it converges to a near-constant
answer. We attribute the collapse to likelihood displacement and the absence of any constraint on the
output distribution, and we fix it with two anchors --- a term holding the chosen answer's likelihood at
or above the frozen base, and an explicit KL penalty to the base on general inputs --- with an ablation
showing both are necessary. With both terms the adapter reliably redirects grounding toward audio: under a
leakage-controlled, paired protocol (same held-out items, McNemar), the video-driven \emph{audio}
hallucination probe rises significantly on both Qwen backbones and on every seed, and the over-reliance
axis agrees. This nets a significant overall hallucination-benchmark gain on Qwen3-Omni, a redistribution
toward audio (at audio--video matching's expense) on Qwen2.5-Omni, and a regression on VideoLLaMA2, which
under-uses audio. A per-token probe shows the learned transform mostly reshapes the vision stream while
the audio path stays a pass-through, so conflict resolution happens inside the frozen language model. We
do not claim a state-of-the-art result; the contribution is the diagnosis, the fix, and an honest,
backbone-dependent grounding study.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Audio--visual large language models couple pretrained audio and vision encoders to a language model
through lightweight per-modality adapters. They exhibit a modality imbalance: they tend to answer from
the visual stream and under-use audio, hallucinating sounds implied by the image and ignoring audio that
conflicts with it~\cite{cmm,avhbench}. We ask whether this can be corrected cheaply, by \emph{freezing the
entire model} and training only a small per-modality variational information bottleneck (VIB) inserted on
the adapter token streams.

The training signal is a modality-conditional preference. From a clean clip we synthesize an
audio-swapped counterfactual (the original video with another clip's audio) and prefer the answer
consistent with the \emph{heard} event over the answer consistent with the \emph{seen} event. Because both
answers are scored on the same swapped clip, preferring the heard event requires using the audio.

We report three things. (i)~Applying DPO to this objective, while regularizing only the bottleneck's
information rate, collapses the model; we attribute it to likelihood displacement and a missing
output-distribution constraint. (ii)~A chosen-likelihood anchor and an explicit KL-to-base penalty prevent
the collapse, and an ablation shows both terms are necessary --- this is our main result. (iii)~With both
terms the adapter redirects grounding toward audio: the audio-hallucination probe improves significantly on
both Qwen backbones, netting an overall gain on Qwen3-Omni, a redistribution on Qwen2.5-Omni, and a
regression on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio. We do not claim state of the art.

The base model is frozen (Figure~\ref{fig:method}). On each per-modality adapter output (the token sequence
$x\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times d}$ feeding the language model) we attach a VIB via a forward hook:
\begin{equation}
z=\mu(x)+\sigma(x)\odot\epsilon,\ \epsilon\sim\mathcal N(0,I);\qquad y=x+W_{\text{out}}z,
\end{equation}
with $W_{\text{out}}$ zero-initialized so $y=x$ at initialization. The per-token rate is
$\KL(q(z|x)\,\|\,\mathcal N(0,I))$. A bypass flag sets $y=x$, recovering the exact frozen base; we use it
for the DPO reference log-probabilities and the KL-to-base target without holding a second model. Only the
two VIBs (audio, vision) are trainable. The bottleneck attaches at each backbone's modality-adapter
outputs: the audio-tower projection and the vision merger for Qwen3-/Qwen2.5-Omni, and the BEATs audio
adapter and SigLIP vision adapter for VideoLLaMA2.1-7B-AV.

\section{Method: objective and losses}
\label{sec:method}
\textbf{Audio-swap preference and the naive objective.} For a clip we build a multiple-choice question and
an audio-swapped copy (video unchanged, audio replaced by a different-category clip). The chosen letter is
the heard event, the rejected letter the seen event, both scored on the swapped clip. Let $c_p,r_p$ be the
policy log-probs of the chosen/rejected letters (bottleneck active) and $c_r,r_r$ the reference log-probs
(bottleneck bypassed). The naive objective regularizes only the latent rate $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L_0=-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:naive}
\end{equation}
Nothing constrains the output distribution against the base. Because $\mathcal L_0$ is a log-sigmoid of a
difference, it can be minimized by lowering both letter log-probs; with an always-on adapter and an
input-blind objective the probability mass moves to a degenerate constant answer.

\textbf{Anchored swap-DPO.} We add two terms:
\begin{align}
\mathcal L=\ &-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)
 +\lambda_{a}\,\big(\!-\log\sigma(\beta(c_p-c_r)-\delta)\big)\nonumber\\
 &+\lambda_{kl}\,\KL\!\big(p_{\text{base}}(\cdot|x)\,\|\,p_\theta(\cdot|x)\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:anchored}
\end{align}
The \emph{chosen anchor} (second term, $\delta{=}0$)~\cite{mdpo,dpop} keeps the chosen log-prob at or above
the frozen base. The \emph{KL-to-base} term penalizes the policy for deviating from the base's answer
distribution on a set of general (non-swap) inputs $x$; because the base is the bypassed bottleneck,
$p_{\text{base}}$ is available without a second model. The general inputs span matched multiple-choice,
audio-presence, visual-presence, and open-ended prompts (the ``broad'' set).

\textbf{Monitoring, selection, and numerics.} Throughout training we log the absolute chosen/rejected log-probs (not
only their margin), $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$, the KL-to-base, and the fraction of ``yes'' answers on a balanced
yes/no probe (a constant-answer detector); we save checkpoints periodically and select on held-out
benchmarks, not the training proxy, maximizing the hallucination benchmark subject to capability guards
(Section~\ref{sec:setup}). The Qwen backbones run in bfloat16; VideoLLaMA2's float16 forward overflows
attention, so we cast it to bfloat16. The VIB is computed in float32 (bfloat16's mantissa is insufficient
for the squared-mean rate term), its input is LayerNorm-normalized for backbones with large adapter
activations, gradients are clipped to norm $1.0$, and its layers are explicitly initialized (the
VideoLLaMA2 loader disables default initialization process-wide). The Qwen backbones are unaffected by
these guards.

\section{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:setup}
\textbf{Backbones:} Qwen3-Omni (primary), Qwen2.5-Omni, VideoLLaMA2.1-7B-AV; all frozen, only the two VIBs
trained. \textbf{Training data:} audio-swapped AVE clips ($300$ preference pairs, two epochs;
$\lambda_a{=}1$, $\lambda_{kl}{=}2$, $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$). \textbf{Selection (leakage control):} checkpoints
and hyperparameters are selected on a $300$-example development split of each benchmark, and \emph{all final
numbers are on the disjoint held-out remainder} (the hallucination benchmark contributing $n{=}5002$ test
items). The rule is the highest validation hallucination accuracy subject to capability guards
$\text{PA}\ge\text{PA}_{\text{base}}-0.05$, $\text{HR}\ge\text{HR}_{\text{base}}-0.05$ (relative to each
backbone's base). \textbf{Answer parsing.} Free-text outputs are mapped to yes/no by a negation-aware
parser (e.g.\ ``I did not see a tree''$\,\to\,$``no''); an earlier negation-blind parser floored
VideoLLaMA2's hallucination-resistance, since it answers in full sentences whereas the Qwen backbones lead
with ``yes''/``no''. We re-derived every metric from saved raw outputs; the correction leaves all Qwen
numbers unchanged. \textbf{Statistics.} Because base and adapted see identical items, every comparison is
a \emph{paired} McNemar test on the discordant pairs, with bootstrap $95\%$ CIs over examples and seed
variance reported in text.

\section{Benchmarks}
\label{sec:bench}
We evaluate on three audio--visual benchmarks. \textbf{AVHBench}~\cite{avhbench} measures cross-modal
hallucination via three yes/no tasks: audio-driven \emph{video} hallucination ($A\!\to\!V$: is a queried
object visible?), video-driven \emph{audio} hallucination ($V\!\to\!A$: is a queried sound audible?), and
audio--video matching; we report overall accuracy and read $V\!\to\!A$ as the direct audio-grounding probe.
\textbf{CMM}~\cite{cmm} separates perception accuracy (PA; affirming present content) from hallucination
resistance (HR; denying absent content) and includes over-reliance subsets (e.g.\
\texttt{overrely\_visual\_ignore\_audio}) that test whether a model ignores one modality for another ---
our most direct over-reliance probe. \textbf{DAVE}~\cite{dave} is an audio--visual multiple-choice
diagnostic. As reference points we also evaluate two frozen frontier baselines: \emph{Gemini} (native
audio--visual) and \emph{GPT-4o} (vision-only, hence audio-blind on non-speech sound).

\section{Results and comparisons}
\label{sec:results}
Table~\ref{tab:main} compares all models (full-set accuracy; held-out paired-McNemar significance below).
The adapter's robust, seed-invariant effect is the video-driven \emph{audio}-hallucination probe
($V\!\to\!A$): it rises on both Qwen backbones (Qwen3-Omni $0.787\to0.815$, Qwen2.5-Omni $0.690\to0.780$),
significantly on \emph{every} seed on the held-out split (paired McNemar, all $p<0.05$), and on the CMM
over-reliance subset that tests ignoring audio the resistance rises on all three backbones --- the model
stops ignoring what it hears. On Qwen3-Omni this nets an overall AVHBench gain ($0.714\to0.757$) that is
significant held-out (pooled across seeds, $p<10^{-4}$), with audio--video matching \emph{also} improving
($0.609\to0.693$). On Qwen2.5-Omni the large audio gain trades against matching ($0.755\to0.682$), so
overall AVHBench barely moves ($0.737\to0.754$, neutral when pooled across seeds) while audio
hallucination-resistance rises (HR $0.512\to0.559$, held-out $p<10^{-4}$): a redistribution toward audio,
not a free gain. On VideoLLaMA2 the adapter \emph{regresses} grounding --- AVHBench $0.715\to0.669$ and the
audio probe $0.766\to0.721$ both fall --- consistent with its weak, separately-aligned audio
pathway~\cite{avllm_seehear,fastav}: there is little audio signal to exploit. The frontier baselines are
reference points: Gemini (native audio--visual) reaches $V\!\to\!A{=}0.747$, which the adapted Qwen3-Omni
($0.815$) leads, while vision-only GPT-4o fails the audio probe ($0.510$) as expected.

\section{Ablations}
\label{sec:abl}
Table~\ref{tab:abl} isolates the two anchors on Qwen3-Omni (development subset). The naive objective
(Eq.~\ref{eq:naive}) collapses the model: trained to convergence it drives CMM perception from $0.939$ to a
near-constant $0.015$ --- the model answers ``no'' to everything, which trivially inflates
hallucination-resistance to $1.0$. The chosen anchor alone delays but does not prevent the perception loss
(falling to $0.303$ at convergence); the KL-to-base alone keeps perception near base but loses
hallucination-resistance ($0.655$). Only with \emph{both} terms does the selected checkpoint keep both
capability axes within tolerance \emph{and} attain the best hallucination accuracy. All four objectives
drift if trained too long; selection takes the checkpoint before this drift, and the choice is insensitive
to the guard threshold. A rate-weight sweep over $\beta_{kl}\in\{0.01,0.1,1.0\}$ leaves the hallucination
accuracy essentially unchanged ($\approx0.70$) while larger weights pass more validation guards, i.e.\
the information-rate penalty buys training stability, not accuracy.

\section{Discussion}
\textbf{What the trained bottleneck does.} A forward-pass probe of the held Qwen3-Omni checkpoint reads
each VIB's per-token rate and edit magnitude $\|W_{\text{out}}z\|/\|x\|$. The vision VIB rewrites the
vision tokens substantially ($\sim$59\% of each token's magnitude) while the audio VIB is close to a
pass-through (a small constant bias; near-zero rate). Grounding is thus achieved by reshaping the
\emph{vision} stream rather than by modifying audio; because each VIB is per-modality, the transform is
unconditional and the conflict resolution occurs inside the frozen language model. The vision rate
($\sim$640 bits/token) is large relative to the loss weight $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ on the mean rate, so the
bottleneck is weakly compressed at this setting --- consistent with the rate sweep.

\textbf{Related work.} DPO~\cite{dpo} maximizes a log-sigmoid of the chosen$-$rejected log-probability gap;
that gap can grow while the \emph{preferred} probability falls (``likelihood displacement''~\cite{razin}),
worst when chosen and rejected are near-identical, and DPO-Positive~\cite{dpop} adds a floor on the chosen
log-probability. DPO's KL constraint is implicit and acts only on the pairs~\cite{dpovsppo}. mDPO~\cite{mdpo}
reports the analogous multimodal failure (the model ignores the image, chosen likelihood drops) and adds a
conditional term and reward anchor; our chosen anchor follows the same principle with audio as the
conditioning modality. Capability-retention work motivates the explicit KL-to-base~\cite{instructgpt,gao}
and selection on diverse held-out evaluations~\cite{ivison}. The variational IB~\cite{vib} trades
sufficiency against a rate penalty; Vittle~\cite{vittle} applies a VIB to multimodal-LLM hidden states. We
place a per-modality VIB on adapter outputs and find the rate penalty, as commonly weighted, weak.

\textbf{Limitations.} The grounding effect is the validation-selected checkpoint, not a monotone
improvement, and it is backbone-dependent: significant overall on Qwen3-Omni, a redistribution (audio up,
matching down; neutral overall) on Qwen2.5-Omni, and a regression on VideoLLaMA2. The broad anchor was
designed after inspecting CMM failures, so CMM is a development signal and a residual test-informed-design
risk remains. The information-bottleneck framing is partly nominal at $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$. The audio-swap
signal is synthetic, the benchmarks are yes/no-heavy (the regime where likelihood displacement bites), and
the training set is small; a larger-data replication and an open-ended slice are the natural next checks.

\textbf{Conclusion.} The robust, transferable result is a diagnosis and a fix: naive preference
optimization of a frozen AV-LLM's adapter streams collapses the model via likelihood displacement, and a
two-anchor loss (a chosen-likelihood anchor plus a KL-to-base on general inputs) prevents it, with an
ablation showing both terms are necessary. The downstream grounding effect is real and backbone-dependent,
and a per-token probe shows it is realized by reshaping the vision stream inside the frozen model. We
release the diagnosis, the recipe, the monitoring/selection and paired-statistics protocol, and the
numerical fixes.
