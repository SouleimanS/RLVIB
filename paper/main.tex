\title{\textbf{Preference-Optimization Collapse in Frozen Audio--Visual LLMs\\
and a Two-Anchor Mitigation}}
\author{Anonymous}
\date{}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We adapt a frozen audio--visual large language model (AV-LLM) by training only a small per-modality
transform on its modality-adapter token streams, with a modality-conditional (audio-swap) preference
objective, and we study what breaks and what holds. Applying direct preference optimization (DPO) to this
setup collapses the model: on a held-out perception benchmark its accuracy falls from $0.953$ to $0.013$
and it converges to a near-constant answer. We attribute the collapse to likelihood displacement and the
absence of any constraint on the output distribution, and we fix it with two anchors --- a term holding
the chosen answer's likelihood at or above the frozen base, and an explicit KL penalty to the base on
general inputs. An ablation shows both are necessary: the first alone still loses perception accuracy, the
second alone loses hallucination-resistance. This diagnosis-and-mitigation is our main, reproducible
result. The downstream grounding effect is real and backbone-dependent: under a leakage-controlled,
\emph{paired} protocol (same held-out test items, McNemar; AVHBench $n{=}5002$), the adapter reliably
improves audio grounding --- the video-driven \emph{audio}-hallucination accuracy rises on both Qwen
backbones and on every seed (Qwen3-Omni $+0.02$ to $+0.03$, Qwen2.5-Omni $+0.06$ to $+0.11$; all
$p<0.05$), and the CMM over-reliance axis agrees. On Qwen3-Omni this nets a significant overall AVHBench
gain ($0.718\to0.761$, pooled $p<10^{-4}$); on Qwen2.5-Omni the audio gain trades against audio--video
matching, so the overall benchmark is neutral pooled; on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio, the adapter
\emph{regresses} grounding. A per-token probe shows the learned transform mostly reshapes the \emph{vision}
stream while the audio path stays a pass-through, so conflict resolution happens inside the frozen LM and
the information-bottleneck rate penalty is nominal at our operating point. Grounding numbers are on the
full held-out test split; the collapse diagnosis uses $300$-example subsets; we report confidence intervals
throughout and discuss limitations in detail.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Audio--visual large language models couple pretrained audio and vision encoders to a language model
through lightweight per-modality adapters. They exhibit a modality imbalance: they tend to answer from
the visual stream and under-use audio, including hallucinating sounds implied by the image and ignoring
audio that conflicts with it~\cite{cmm,avhbench}. We ask whether this can be corrected cheaply, by
\emph{freezing the entire model} and training only a small per-modality variational information
bottleneck (VIB) inserted on the adapter token streams.

The training signal is a modality-conditional preference. From a clean clip we synthesize an
audio-swapped counterfactual (the original video with another clip's audio) and prefer the answer
consistent with the \emph{heard} event over the answer consistent with the \emph{seen} event. Because
both answers are scored on the same swapped clip, preferring the heard event requires using the audio.

We report three things. (i)~Applying DPO to this objective, while regularizing only the bottleneck's
information rate, collapses the model (Section~\ref{sec:method-anchored}); we attribute the collapse to
likelihood displacement and a missing output-distribution constraint. (ii)~A chosen-likelihood anchor and
an explicit KL-to-base penalty prevent the collapse, and an ablation shows both terms are necessary
(Section~\ref{sec:abl}); this is our main result. (iii)~With both terms the adapter reliably redirects
grounding toward audio --- the video-driven audio-hallucination probe rises significantly on both Qwen
backbones across every seed, and the CMM over-reliance axis agrees --- netting a significant overall
AVHBench gain on Qwen3-Omni, a redistribution (audio up, audio--video matching down) on Qwen2.5-Omni, and a
regression on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio (Section~\ref{sec:results}); a per-token probe shows the
learned transform reshapes the vision stream rather than the audio path, with the frozen language model
resolving the conflict (Section~\ref{sec:interp}). We do not claim a state-of-the-art result.

The base model is frozen (Figure~\ref{fig:method}). On each per-modality adapter output (the token sequence
$x\in\mathbb{R}^{T\times d}$ feeding the language model) we attach a VIB via a forward hook:
\begin{equation}
z=\mu(x)+\sigma(x)\odot\epsilon,\ \epsilon\sim\mathcal N(0,I);\qquad y=x+W_{\text{out}}z,
\end{equation}
with $W_{\text{out}}$ zero-initialized so $y=x$ at initialization. The per-token rate is
$\KL(q(z|x)\,\|\,\mathcal N(0,I))$. A bypass flag sets $y=x$, recovering the exact frozen base; we use it
for the DPO reference log-probabilities and the KL-to-base target without holding a second model. Only
the two VIBs (audio, vision) are trainable. Concretely, the bottleneck attaches at each backbone's
modality-adapter output: for Qwen3-/Qwen2.5-Omni the audio tower projection and the vision merger, and for
VideoLLaMA2.1-7B-AV the BEATs audio adapter and the SigLIP vision adapter feeding the language model.

\section{Method: objective and losses}

\subsection{Audio-swap preference and the naive objective}
\label{sec:collapse}
For a clip we build a multiple-choice question and an audio-swapped copy (video unchanged, audio replaced
by a different-category clip). The chosen letter is the heard event, the rejected letter the seen event,
both scored on the swapped clip. Let $c_p,r_p$ be the policy log-probs of the chosen/rejected letters
(bottleneck active) and $c_r,r_r$ the reference log-probs (bottleneck bypassed). The naive objective
regularizes only the latent rate $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal L_0=-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:naive}
\end{equation}
Nothing constrains the output distribution against the base. Because $\mathcal L_0$ is a log-sigmoid of a
difference, it can be minimized by lowering both letter log-probs; with an always-on adapter and an
input-blind objective the probability mass moves to a degenerate constant answer.

\subsection{Anchored swap-DPO}
\label{sec:method-anchored}
We add two terms:
\begin{align}
\mathcal L=\ &-\log\sigma\!\big(\beta[(c_p-c_r)-(r_p-r_r)]\big)
 +\lambda_{a}\,\big(\!-\log\sigma(\beta(c_p-c_r)-\delta)\big)\nonumber\\
 &+\lambda_{kl}\,\KL\!\big(p_{\text{base}}(\cdot|x)\,\|\,p_\theta(\cdot|x)\big)+\beta_{kl}\,\KL_{\text{VIB}}.
\label{eq:anchored}
\end{align}
The \emph{chosen anchor} (second term, $\delta{=}0$)~\cite{mdpo,dpop} keeps the chosen log-prob at or above
the frozen base. The \emph{KL-to-base} term penalizes the policy for deviating from the base's answer
distribution on a set of general (non-swap) inputs $x$; because the base is the bypassed bottleneck,
$p_{\text{base}}$ is available without a second model. The general inputs span matched multiple-choice,
audio-presence, visual-presence, and open-ended prompts (the ``broad'' set).

\subsection{Monitoring and selection}
Per step we log the absolute chosen/rejected log-probs (not only their margin), $\KL_{\text{VIB}}$, the
KL-to-base, and the fraction of ``yes'' answers on a balanced yes/no probe (a constant-answer detector).
Checkpoints are saved periodically and selected on the held-out benchmarks, not the training proxy. The
selection rule maximizes AVHBench subject to capability guards (Section~\ref{sec:setup}).

\subsection{Implementation details (numerical)}
\label{sec:impl}
The Qwen-Omni backbones run in bfloat16. VideoLLaMA2 is distributed in float16 for inference but trained
in bfloat16; a differentiable float16 forward overflows attention, so we cast it to bfloat16. The VIB is
computed in float32 (bfloat16's mantissa is insufficient for the squared-mean term in the rate), its
encoder input is LayerNorm-normalized for backbones with large adapter activations, gradients are clipped
to norm $1.0$, and its linear layers are explicitly initialized (the VideoLLaMA2 loader disables the
default initialization process-wide). The Qwen backbones are unaffected by these guards.

\section{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:setup}
\textbf{Backbones:} Qwen3-Omni (primary), Qwen2.5-Omni, VideoLLaMA2.1-7B-AV; all frozen, only the
two VIBs trained. \textbf{Training data:} audio-swapped AVE clips (300 pairs, 2 epochs, $\beta{=}0.1$,
$\lambda_a{=}1$, $\lambda_{kl}{=}2$, $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ unless stated). \textbf{Selection protocol
(leakage control):} checkpoints (and the recipe and $\beta_{kl}$) are selected on a $300$-example
development split of each benchmark (internally val/test, used for the collapse diagnosis), and \emph{all
final numbers are on the disjoint held-out remainder} (records$[300{:}]$; AVHBench $n{=}5002$, CMM
$n{=}1050$). The selection rule is the highest validation AVHBench subject to capability guards
$\text{PA}\ge\text{PA}_{\text{base}}-0.05$ and $\text{HR}\ge\text{HR}_{\text{base}}-0.05$ (relative to
each backbone's base, computed on the development split). The ablation and rate-sweep tables
(\ref{tab:abl},~\ref{tab:beta}) are on the $300$-subset test half (the diagnosis protocol); the seed,
per-task, and multi-model tables (\ref{tab:seeds},~\ref{tab:avhtask},~\ref{tab:models}) are on the full
held-out remainder.
\textbf{Answer parsing.} Free-text outputs are mapped to yes/no by a parser that recognizes negated
absence answers (e.g.\ ``I did not see a tree''$\,\to\,$``no''). An earlier negation-blind parser scored
such answers as unparseable errors, which floored VideoLLaMA2's CMM hallucination-resistance to near
zero; VideoLLaMA2 answers in full sentences, whereas the Qwen backbones answer with a leading
``yes''/``no'' and are unaffected. We corrected the parser and re-derived every metric from the saved raw
outputs; the correction leaves all Qwen2.5-/Qwen3-Omni numbers unchanged, isolating the change to the one
backbone that answered in prose.
\textbf{Development vs.\ test, and paired statistics.} The \emph{design} of the broad anchor was informed
by inspecting CMM perception/hallucination failures on the held-out benchmarks, so we read CMM as a
\emph{development} signal and treat AVHBench as the primary held-out test; the checkpoint, recipe and
$\beta_{kl}$ are still selected only on the validation half, but a residual test-informed-design risk
remains, which we flag rather than claim away. Because base and adapted see identical items, every
base-vs-adapted comparison below is a \emph{paired} McNemar test on the discordant pairs, with bootstrap
$95\%$ CIs over examples reported alongside the seed variance.

\section{Benchmarks}
\label{sec:bench}
We evaluate on three audio--visual benchmarks. \textbf{AVHBench}~\cite{avhbench} ($5302$ items) measures
cross-modal hallucination via three yes/no tasks: audio-driven \emph{video} hallucination ($A\!\to\!V$: is
a queried object visible?), video-driven \emph{audio} hallucination ($V\!\to\!A$: is a queried sound
audible?), and audio--video matching; we report overall and per-task accuracy and read $V\!\to\!A$ as the
direct audio-grounding probe. \textbf{CMM}~\cite{cmm} ($2400$ items) separates perception accuracy (PA;
correctly affirming present content) from hallucination resistance (HR; correctly denying absent content),
and includes over-reliance subsets (e.g.\ \texttt{overrely\_visual\_ignore\_audio}) that test whether a
model ignores one modality in favour of another --- our most direct over-reliance probe.
\textbf{DAVE}~\cite{dave} ($1572$ items) is an audio--visual multiple-choice diagnostic. All three are
split into a $300$-example development portion (for selection) and a disjoint held-out remainder (for
reporting), as in Section~\ref{sec:setup}.

\section{Results and comparisons}
\label{sec:results}
Because base and adapted are evaluated on identical test items, we report a paired McNemar test on the
discordant pairs rather than comparing two independently-noisy point estimates (Table~\ref{tab:seeds}), on
the full held-out test split (records$[300{:}]$; AVHBench $n{=}5002$). The per-seed AVHBench gain is
$+0.043/+0.008/+0.021$ (discordant $b/c=133/346,\,243/281,\,138/243$; $p<\!10^{-4},\,0.11,\,<\!10^{-4}$):
significant for two of three seeds and significant pooled ($b{=}514$, $c{=}870$, $p<\!10^{-4}$), and a
bootstrap over test examples gives $\Delta=+0.043\,[{+}0.034,{+}0.051]$ for the selected seed against a base
of $0.718\,[0.706,0.731]$. The seed-invariant component is the video-driven \emph{audio}-hallucination
probe ($V\!\to\!A$: $+0.026/+0.021/+0.020$, all three significant; Table~\ref{tab:avhtask}) --- the model
grounds better in what it hears. The selected model is not collapsed (yes-rate $0.67$ on a balanced probe),
and the selection is insensitive to the guard threshold (the same checkpoint is chosen at
$\mathrm{tol}\in\{0.03,0.05,0.08\}$). The capability axes are near-stable (CMM-PA $-0.009$, $p=0.14$; CMM-HR
$+0.001$, $p=1.0$ for the selected seed), and we treat CMM as a development signal
(Section~\ref{sec:setup}). The $\pm0.018$ in Table~\ref{tab:seeds} is seed variance only and understates
the per-example noise.

\subsection{All three backbones}
Table~\ref{tab:models} reports all three backbones; the Qwen rows are on the full held-out test split. The
effect that transfers across backbones is the \emph{audio probe}: video-driven audio-hallucination accuracy
rises on both Qwen models and is significant on \emph{every} seed (Qwen3-Omni $+0.020$ to $+0.026$;
Qwen2.5-Omni $+0.064$ to $+0.108$; all $p<0.05$), and the CMM over-reliance axis agrees --- on the full set
the \texttt{overrely\_visual\_ignore\_audio} resistance rises on all three backbones ($0.57\to0.61$,
$0.21\to0.33$, $0.47\to0.56$), i.e.\ the adapter makes a frozen model stop ignoring audio. On Qwen3-Omni
this nets a significant overall AVHBench gain ($0.718\to0.761$; pooled $b{=}514$, $c{=}870$, $p<10^{-4}$)
with AV-matching \emph{also} improving ($+0.084$). On Qwen2.5-Omni the audio gain is large
($V\!\to\!A\;{+}0.087$) but trades against AV-matching ($-0.072$): the selected checkpoint's overall
AVHBench is positive and significant ($0.745\to0.759$, $p=0.005$), yet pooled across seeds it is neutral
($b{=}1254$, $c{=}1217$, $p=0.47$) --- a redistribution toward audio, not a free gain --- and its clearest
capability win is hallucination-resistance on audio (CMM-HR $0.491\to0.551$, $p<10^{-4}$). On VideoLLaMA2
the adapter \emph{regresses} grounding: full-set AVHBench $0.715\to0.669$, the audio probe $0.766\to0.721$,
and DAVE $0.296\to0.198$ (its over-reliance resistance still nudges up). This is consistent with
VideoLLaMA2's weak, separately-aligned audio pathway: its audio behavior largely inherits its
vision-language base model~\cite{avllm_seehear} and its audio tokens prune almost entirely without loss of
accuracy~\cite{fastav} --- there is little audio signal for the adapter to exploit, so reshaping the streams
mostly hurts. A held-out paired test for VideoLLaMA2 and the per-token audio-VIB probe of
Section~\ref{sec:interp} --- the cleanest internal check of ``no audio signal to exploit'' --- are the
natural confirmations and we leave them to follow-up. Base DAVE (MCQ) accuracies are $0.326/0.246/0.296$
for Qwen3-/Qwen2.5-Omni/VideoLLaMA2. (VideoLLaMA2's high CMM hallucination-resistance is the corrected
measurement of Section~\ref{sec:setup}, ``Answer parsing''; an earlier draft scored it near zero.)

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{What the trained bottleneck does}
\label{sec:interp}
A forward-pass probe of the held Qwen3-Omni checkpoint reads each VIB's per-token rate (KL in bits) and
edit magnitude ($\|W_{\text{out}}z\|/\|x\|$); Table~\ref{tab:interp} reports averages over 8 clips. The
vision VIB rewrites the vision tokens substantially ($\sim$59\% of each token's magnitude, spread across
all tokens) while the audio VIB is close to a pass-through (its small edit is a constant bias; its rate is
near zero). The grounding is thus achieved by reshaping the vision stream rather than by modifying audio;
because each VIB is per-modality, this transform is unconditional and the conflict resolution occurs in
the frozen language model. The vision rate ($\sim$640 bits/token) is large relative to the loss weight
$\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ applied to the \emph{mean} rate, i.e. the bottleneck is weakly compressed at this
setting --- consistent with the $\beta_{kl}$ sweep, where larger weights stabilize training.

\subsection{Related work}
\textbf{Preference optimization and its failure modes.} DPO~\cite{dpo} optimizes a log-sigmoid of the
difference between chosen and rejected log-probabilities. This difference can be increased by lowering
both, and the probability of the \emph{preferred} response can fall --- ``likelihood
displacement''~\cite{razin} --- worst when chosen and rejected are near-identical. DPO-Positive~\cite{dpop}
adds a penalty preventing the chosen log-probability from dropping below the reference. Unlike PPO, DPO's
KL constraint is implicit and acts only on the preference pairs~\cite{dpovsppo}.
\textbf{Multimodal preference optimization.} mDPO~\cite{mdpo} reports an analogous failure on multimodal
LLMs (the model ignores the image and the chosen likelihood drops) and adds a conditional term and a
reward anchor; our chosen anchor follows the same principle, with audio as the conditioning modality.
\textbf{Capability retention.} RLHF imposes an ``alignment tax''; explicit KL-to-reference penalties and
pretraining-mixing reduce it~\cite{instructgpt}, and proxy over-optimization past a KL budget degrades
true quality~\cite{gao}; selection should use diverse held-out evaluations~\cite{ivison}.
\textbf{Information bottleneck.} The variational IB~\cite{vib} trades sufficiency against a rate penalty;
Vittle~\cite{vittle} applies a VIB to multimodal-LLM hidden states. We use a per-modality VIB on adapter
outputs and report that the rate penalty, as commonly weighted, is weak (Section~\ref{sec:interp}).

\subsection{Limitations}
Selection uses a $300$-example validation subset, but final metrics are the \emph{full} held-out test split
(records$[300{:}]$; AVHBench $n{=}5002$, CMM $n{=}1050$); we lead with paired McNemar tests and bootstrap
CIs rather than bare point estimates, and report seed variance separately. The grounding gain is
backbone-dependent: a robust, significant audio-probe gain on both Qwen backbones (every seed) that nets a
significant overall AVHBench gain on Qwen3-Omni (pooled $p<10^{-4}$), only a redistribution on Qwen2.5-Omni
(audio up, matching down; overall neutral pooled, $p=0.47$), and a regression on VideoLLaMA2. It is the
validation-selected checkpoint, not a monotone training improvement --- all recipes drift if trained
longer. As stated in Section~\ref{sec:setup}, the broad anchor was designed after inspecting CMM failures,
so CMM is a development signal and a residual test-informed-design risk remains. The information-bottleneck
framing is partly nominal: at $\beta_{kl}{=}0.01$ the rate penalty is weak and the learned transform
reshapes the vision stream rather than compressing audio. We audit clip-id overlap between the AVE training
pool and the three benchmarks to check dataset-level leakage. Finally, the audio-swap signal is synthetic,
the benchmarks are yes/no-heavy (the regime where likelihood displacement bites), and the training set is
small ($300$ pairs, two epochs); a larger-data replication and a non-yes/no (open-ended) slice are the
natural next checks.

\subsection{Conclusion}
The robust, transferable result of this paper is a diagnosis and a fix: naive preference optimization of a
frozen AV-LLM's adapter streams collapses the model via likelihood displacement, and a two-anchor loss
(a chosen-likelihood anchor plus a KL-to-base on general inputs) prevents it, with an ablation showing
both terms are necessary. The downstream grounding effect is real and backbone-dependent --- the
audio-hallucination probe improves significantly on both Qwen backbones (every seed), netting a significant
overall AVHBench gain on Qwen3-Omni, a redistribution toward audio (at audio--video matching's expense) on
Qwen2.5-Omni, and a regression on VideoLLaMA2, which under-uses audio. A per-token probe shows the learned
transform mostly reshapes the vision stream, so the conflict is resolved inside the frozen language model
and the bottleneck's rate penalty is nominal at our operating point. We release the diagnosis, the recipe,
the monitoring, selection, and paired-statistics protocol, and the numerical fixes.
